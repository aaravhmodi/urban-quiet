# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Urban residents, students, and commuters who want to understand noise conditions in their city. Secondary users: city planning researchers and public health academics. They're on their phone, outdoors, in the middle of doing something else. The app should feel like a civic utility — quick to use, easy to trust.

## Product Purpose

UrbanQuiet lets anyone record a short ambient sound sample, tag it with their location, and contribute it to a crowdsourced city noise map. The product surfaces quiet zones, noisy areas, and trends over time. Success means: a user can submit a sample in under 30 seconds and find a quiet area in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Calm, civic, trustworthy. Voice: quiet confidence. Tone: clear and direct, never playful or gamified. Like a well-designed city data portal — but alive with real-time contribution.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark SaaS (slate-900 + blue buttons — indistinguishable from every other tool)
- Gamified apps with badges and bright icon grids
- Government portal aesthetics — dense, bureaucratic, plain

## Design Principles

1. **The map is the product.** Every page serves the map or feeds data into it.
2. **Quiet confidence.** Restraint over noise — visually and conceptually.
3. **Civic clarity.** Data presented so anyone can act on it immediately, no legend required.
4. **Trust through precision.** Use exact labels, accurate caveats, no hype. "Estimated loudness" not "decibel level."
5. **Mobile-first, task-first.** Every interaction optimized for one hand, outdoors, in motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Color not used as the sole signal (loudness also shown as text/number). Reduced motion respected.
